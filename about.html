About Tyrrell County

Tyrrell County is a rural county located in the northeastern part of North Carolina. It covers an area of approximately 594 square miles and is bordered by the Albemarle Sound to the south and Washington County to the north.

The county seat is Columbia, a charming town with a population of around 900 residents. Columbia is known for its historic downtown area, which features beautifully preserved buildings dating back to the 19th century.

Our History

Founded in 1729, Tyrrell County has a rich history that spans centuries. The area was originally inhabited by Native American tribes, including the Chowanoke and Weapemeoc peoples. European settlers arrived in the late 17th century, establishing farms and plantations along the region's fertile land.

During the Civil War, Tyrrell County played a strategic role due to its proximity to the Albemarle Sound and the vital waterways of eastern North Carolina. Today, visitors can explore historic sites such as Somerset Place, a former antebellum plantation that offers a glimpse into the area's plantation economy.

Our Mission

At Tyrrell County, our mission is to preserve and enhance the quality of life for all residents by providing efficient and effective public services, fostering economic development, and promoting environmental stewardship.

We strive to create a welcoming and inclusive community where everyone can thrive, and we are committed to transparency, accountability, and collaboration in all our endeavors.
